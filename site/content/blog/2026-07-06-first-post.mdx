The best productivity tools do not feel like AI products. They feel like documents that happen to be alive.

When I sit down to write a weekly dump in Merit, I am not "talking to AI." I am filling out a record. The structuring happens in the background. The output looks like something I would hand my manager — numbered entries, impact bullets, dates in monospace.

That is the bar I keep coming back to: **make the machine invisible**.

## What inspires me right now

- **Editorial typography in software** — serif display type, hairline rules, warm paper backgrounds. Interfaces that respect reading.
- **Specific jobs, not platforms** — Merit does career evidence. FollowThrough does commitments. Relay does context reload. Each one refuses to be everything.
- **Git as publishing** — this site is markdown files in a repo. No CMS, no database. Write, commit, deploy.

## A note on taste

There is a default aesthetic that every LLM reaches for: purple gradients, three feature cards, Inter font, centered heroes. I am actively trying to build against that gravity.

Warm paper. Left-aligned columns. Numbered lists instead of card grids. The site should feel like a table of contents, not a landing page template.

That is what I mean by inspiring me at the moment — not a mood board of references, but a set of constraints that make the work more interesting.
